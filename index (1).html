Who I Am

About Me

I am an undergraduate student of Animal Science with a growing interest in animal genetics, bioinformatics, and data-driven livestock research. I am currently building skills in programming, computational biology, and scientific research with the long-term goal of pursuing graduate studies and contributing to sustainable animal breeding and genetics.

My academic path has been shaped by a genuine curiosity about the genetic underpinnings of animal traits and the ways computational tools can accelerate scientific discovery. I believe the most impactful research lives at the crossroads of disciplines — and I am working every day to stand confidently at that crossroads.

Beyond the laboratory and the terminal window, I am an avid reader, a wildlife enthusiast, and someone who finds creative expression through drawing, photography, and music.

What Drives My Work

Research Interests

🧬

Animal Genetics

Understanding hereditary mechanisms, gene expression, and genetic variation in livestock and companion animals.

🔬

Genomics

Exploring whole-genome sequencing, SNP analysis, and population genomics to characterize genetic diversity in animal populations.

💻

Computational Biology

Applying algorithms and computational models to decode complex biological systems and large-scale genomic datasets.

📊

Bioinformatics

Developing and using bioinformatics pipelines for sequence alignment, variant calling, and functional annotation.

🐄

Animal Breeding

Studying selection strategies, breeding programs, and marker-assisted selection for improved livestock performance.

📐

Quantitative Genetics

Investigating the genetic architecture of complex traits using heritability estimation, BLUP models, and GWAS approaches.

🌱

Sustainable Livestock Production

Exploring genetic and management strategies that improve productivity while reducing the environmental footprint of animal agriculture.

📡

Precision Livestock Farming

Using sensors, data analytics, and AI to monitor individual animal health, behaviour, and productivity in real time.

📈

Data Science in Animal Science

Applying machine learning and statistical modelling to predict phenotypic outcomes, disease risk, and genetic merit from biological data.
